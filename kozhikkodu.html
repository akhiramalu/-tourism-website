Located on the northern side of the scenic state of Kerala, Kozhikode has always been a popular destination for its backwaters. Mananchira

Mananchira square is a man-made freshwater pond park situated in the centre of the city of Kozhikode (Calicut) in Kerala, southern India. .

Kariyathumpara

Kariathumpara is a beautiful cool place, almost like a hill station. It is in Kozhikode District of Kerala, and is just 45 kilometers away from the Kozhikode .. It is on the way to famous Kakkayam dam. Unfortunately many of the people just pass this land without knowing about it on their way to Kakkayam. This place is so favorite for many photographers because it gifts them an awesome beauty to capture and also helps in outdoor shooting


Thamarasseri churam

Thamarassery Churam, also known as Thamarassery Ghat Pass, is a captivating mountain pass in Kerala's Western Ghats, connecting Kozhikode with Wayanad. Renowned for its winding roads, lush greenery, and breathtaking views, this ghat pass is a blend of natural beauty, historical significance, and thrilling adventure, making it a popular route for travelers and nature enthusiasts alike.

Kappad

Located in the Kozhikode district of Kerala, Kappad Beach stands out for its historical importance and serene beauty. Known as the landing site of Portuguese explorer Vasco da Gama on May 20, 1498, this beach marked the start of European trade in India. With its golden sands, calm waters, and cultural significance, Kappad offers a unique blend of history, biodiversity, and scenic charm.

Kallai

The village of Kallayi is situated on the banks of the river Kallayi. It has an archaic charm and was a busy timber trade centre during the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries. Today, only a few mills exist in the area but the view from the Kallayi Bridge built by the British is a perfect spot to witness a breathtaking sunset.

Arippara Waterfalls

Arippara Waterfalls is a picturesque hiking area featuring multi-level waterfalls, rocky pools, and a suspension bridge offering stunning river views. Despite being visited on a Sunday, the place wasn't crowded, and entry fees were reasonable at 10 rupees per head. While the water level was low during the visit, it's still an enjoyable spot for photography and leisurely exploration.
