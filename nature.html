Beauty of Kerala



Kerala’s ecosystems offer a diverse array of flora and fauna, inviting nature enthusiasts to explore its abundant wildlife. Periyar Wildlife Sanctuary, nestled in the Cardamom Hills, is one of India’s premier protected areas, home to elephants, tigers, and exotic bird species. A boat safari on Periyar Lake offers visitors a rare chance to witness elephants bathing along the shore and the occasional glimpse of an elusive Bengal tiger. The forests here, rich in medicinal plants and herbs, have nurtured Kerala’s Ayurvedic tradition for centuries. Not only is Periyar a sanctuary for animals, but it also serves as a green pharmacy for Ayurvedic healers who collect plants to create natural medicines. Exploring these forests provides a glimpse into how deeply nature and wellness are interwoven in Kerala’s culture. Birdwatchers will find paradise in the Thattekad Bird Sanctuary, home to over 300 bird species, including the Malabar trogon and Sri Lanka frogmouth. Visitors can wander along forest trails with expert guides who help spot elusive species, offering a peaceful and meditative way to connect with Kerala’s natural bounty.




Hill station


The Kerala hill stations are year-round tourist destinations for offering amazingly balanced and comfortable weather. You can also experience the incredible wildlife in this lush state in southern India. While enjoying boating on charming lakes like the Periyar and Kundala Lake, a glimpse of the exciting wildlife is a common enjoyment. The dramatic hills of the Western Ghat also make for many trekking trails that create great adventure opportunities for travelers. Though famous for its backwater and Arabian coast, what famed Kerala as the ‘God’s own land’ is the lush beauty offered by the Western Ghat. The famous hill stations of Kerala like Munnar, Idduky, Vagamon, Wayanad, and Thekkady are blessed with the unique natural elegance of misty mountains while quaint rivers add more attractions to it. A popular hill station Vyithiri is situated in the Wayanad district of Kerala. This beautiful hill station is famous for the spice plantation that spread a spicy aroma along with the eye-catching view of the greenery. You can also interact with the tribal community during a tour to Vyithiri. This hill station is also home to many charming lakes like Karalad Lake and Pookot Lake. Both are flourished with the evergreen forests that surround the lakes. The Pookot Lake is ideal for adventure lovers where you can enjoy Kayaking, pedal boating and row boating on the lake. There is also a children's park, spice emporium and handicraft market that attract the tourists in mass. A scenic place Lakkidi is located 700m above sea level from where you can enjoy the picturesque views. You can reach there by a drive through the windy and misty road rolling the mountain. :





Beaches


Kerala's landscape is adorned top to bottom by a plethora of beaches that cater to locals and visitors alike. The view on offer is magical and these draw in large crowds to simply relax by the shore or go for a soothing swim. There are numerous water activities and sports at these beaches for those seeking adventure. One also gets to taste regional cuisine at its finest in the shacks lined up alongside these beaches. Backwater


The backwaters in Kerala are one of its most important tourist attractions. They are a series of interconnected lakes and rivers that run parallel to the coast, and their calm waters make for a perfect spot to relax and escape the hustle and bustle of city life. What’s more, the backwaters are home to various wildlife species. If you’re looking for a unique and relaxing holiday experience, then a trip to the backwaters of Kerala is a must!








Waterfalls


Kerala is one of the Indian states that are rich with not just cultural heritage but also amazing landscapes and dazzling waterfalls. Apart from waterfalls, flora and fauna surrounding them give you pristine views of true natural beauty. Blessed with some of the best waterfalls in India, Kerala tops the tourist destination list in India. There are around 27 waterfalls in Kerala, which justifies why this place is called God’s own country. The largest waterfall in Kerala is the Athirapally falls, which stands 80ft tall and appears in various Indian movies.
